## Grounds — the requested records are public records

The records sought are session-level meter and meterUP transaction logs,
residential permit issuances, and on-street parking citations. These are
**operational records of a Commonwealth authority** documenting the use of a
public asset (the curbside) and the assessment of public fees and fines. They
fall squarely within the presumption of public access in § 67.301.

The Office of Open Records has previously held that:

- Aggregate operational data from public agencies is not exempt under
  § 67.708(b)(17) (records identifying an individual) where personally
  identifying fields can be redacted or hashed (see *Bagwell v. Pennsylvania
  Department of Education*, OOR Dkt. AP 2011-0344).
- Cost or processing burden alone does not defeat the public-record
  presumption; agencies are entitled to reasonable fees but may not deny on
  burden grounds where redaction is feasible (§ 67.706, § 67.1308).

I have **expressly offered to accept hashed or redacted plate identifiers**,
removing the only plausible § 67.708(b)(17) concern. I have also offered to
narrow the scope and accept the data in whatever format is least burdensome
for PPA to produce.

## Address PPA's stated grounds

[*If PPA cited specific exemptions, address each here. Common ones to expect:*]

- **§ 67.708(b)(17) — records identifying an individual.** Addressed by
  hashing plate identifiers, which the request explicitly permits.
- **§ 67.708(b)(2) — records related to law enforcement.** Citation records
  are administrative penalties, not law-enforcement investigations.
  Even if any record is treated as such, redaction of any specific
  enforcement-action identifier is sufficient under § 67.706.
- **§ 67.708(b)(11) — trade secrets / confidential proprietary information**
  (likely if PPA cites Flowbird / Parkmobile contracts). Transaction data
  generated by the public's payments at public meters is the Authority's
  data, not the vendor's; vendor pricing or formula confidentiality can be
  redacted without withholding the underlying transaction records.
- **Burden / volume.** Volume is not a basis for denial under the RTKL.
  § 67.1308 governs fee disputes; § 67.703 narrowness concerns can be
  resolved by scoping (which I have offered).

## Requested relief

I respectfully ask that the Office:

1. Find that the requested transaction, permit, and citation records are
   public records under § 67.301 and § 67.302.
2. Order the Philadelphia Parking Authority to produce the records, with
   redaction of any personally identifying fields, within the time required
   by 65 P.S. § 67.1101(b)(1).
3. Award reasonable costs in the event of bad-faith denial (§ 67.1304).
