# Cover Letter — to Council Office

**Date**: _______________

**To**: Chief of Staff, Office of Councilmember [Name]
**From**: Russell Richie, Progress and Poverty Institute / 5th Square
**Re**: Coordinated Right-to-Know request to the Philadelphia Parking Authority
in support of demand-responsive curb pricing research

Dear [Name],

I'm writing on behalf of the Progress and Poverty Institute and 5th Square to
request your office's help in obtaining bulk parking-meter, residential-permit,
and citation data from the Philadelphia Parking Authority via the
Pennsylvania Right-to-Know Law.

**The research.** We are building a quantitative model of demand-responsive
curb parking pricing for Philadelphia, modeled on Seattle's transparent annual-
adjustment program and informed by the Pittsburgh Lawrenceville pilot. The
output supports three deliverables: a 5th Square / PPI white paper on land-
rent capture for the curb, an academic paper testing whether peak-vacancy
targeting outperforms average-occupancy targeting (the Chatman–Manville
critique), and an open-source policy simulation tool that City Council and
PPA could use to evaluate rate changes.

**Why we need PPA data.** The model currently runs on synthetic transaction
stubs, which is sufficient to demonstrate methodology but cannot produce
defensible numerical recommendations for any specific zone or rate change.
Even one calendar year of session-level meter data — already collected by
PPA's payment vendors — would let us calibrate elasticities, validate
occupancy estimates, and produce credible revenue forecasts.

**Why we're asking for your help.** PPA has not historically released bulk
transaction data on request. A coordinated RTKL request from a Council office,
or one that your office endorses on transmittal, carries significantly more
institutional weight than an individual researcher's request. Three options:

1. **Submit on Council letterhead** with our scope language. We will provide
   technical input on what to ask for and the form.
2. **Endorse on transmittal** — i.e., we submit, you co-sign or send a brief
   accompanying note flagging the research significance.
3. **Introduce us to PPA's Government Affairs lead** so we can negotiate
   scope before any formal submission.

The substantive request is short — meter / meterUP transactions (with hashed
plates, session-level), RPP permit issuance, and citation data, for the most
recent two years, in whatever electronic format imposes the lowest processing
cost on PPA. We are explicitly willing to scope down to a single zone-year if
that addresses any burden concerns.

**Distributional argument.** Under Acts 9/39/84, on-street parking revenue
above the $35M General Fund minimum flows to the School District of
Philadelphia. Properly priced curb parking — even at modest demand-elasticity
estimates — could plausibly direct several million dollars more to SDP per
year while reducing cruising VMT in Center City. Rigorous analysis of those
numbers requires the data we're asking for.

If your office is open to discussing, I'd appreciate a 20-minute call. I'm
also happy to share the working model and synthetic outputs in advance.

Best regards,

Russell Richie
